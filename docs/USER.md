# User Guide

## Overview

The ROE/EOE Assessment is an organizational effectiveness survey that measures how well your organization supports results-oriented work practices. The survey takes approximately 15-30 minutes to complete depending on the number of questions assigned.

## Getting Started

### Accessing the Survey

1. You will receive a unique link via email from your organization
2. Click the link to open the survey in your web browser
3. The survey works on desktop, tablet, and mobile devices

### Supported Browsers
- Google Chrome (recommended)
- Mozilla Firefox
- Microsoft Edge
- Safari

## Taking the Survey

### Step 1: Validation

When you open the survey link, the system will automatically validate your invitation. You will see:
- A spinning indicator while validation is in progress
- A success message if your link is valid
- An error message if there's an issue with your link

### Step 2: Personal Information

Depending on your organization's configuration, you may be asked to provide some or all of the following information:

- First Name
- Last Name
- Company
- Position
- Department
- Age
- Years in Company
- Gender
- City
- Country
- Email
- Phone

**Note:** Only fields required by your organization will be displayed.

### Step 3: Survey Instructions

Before starting, you can view the survey instructions by clicking the help icon. The instructions explain:
- The purpose of the survey
- How to answer questions
- Confidentiality information

### Step 4: Answering Questions

Each question presents a statement about your organization. For each statement:

1. Read the statement carefully
2. Consider how accurately it describes your organization
3. Click one of two options:
   - **Yes** - The statement accurately describes your organization
   - **No** - The statement does not accurately describe your organization

### Navigation

- **Previous Button** - Go back to review or change a previous answer
- **Next Button** - Move to the next question (enabled after answering)
- **Progress Bar** - Shows how many questions you've completed

### Step 5: Submission

After answering all questions:
1. Review your progress on the summary screen
2. Click "Submit" to send your responses
3. Wait for confirmation that your submission was successful

## Features

### Auto-Save

Your progress is automatically saved as you complete the survey. If you:
- Close your browser accidentally
- Lose internet connection
- Need to take a break

Simply return to the same link to continue where you left off.

### Resume Progress

When returning to an incomplete survey:
- The system detects your saved progress
- You'll see an option to "Continue Test"
- Your previous answers are preserved
- Question randomization remains the same

### Question Randomization

Questions are presented in a random order that is unique to your session. This randomization is preserved if you resume the survey later.

## Troubleshooting

### "Invalid Test Link" Error
- Verify you're using the exact link provided to you
- Check that the link hasn't expired
- Contact your administrator for a new link

### "Test Already Completed" Message
- This survey has already been submitted
- Each link can only be used once
- Contact your administrator if you need to retake the survey

### Survey Won't Load
- Check your internet connection
- Try refreshing the page
- Clear your browser cache
- Try a different browser

### Progress Not Saved
- Ensure you have a stable internet connection
- Don't use private/incognito browsing mode
- Check that your browser allows localStorage

### Can't Submit Results
- Verify your internet connection
- Try clicking submit again
- If issues persist, your results are saved locally
- Contact your administrator for assistance

## Privacy & Confidentiality

- Your individual responses are confidential
- Results are typically reported in aggregate
- Personal information is used only for survey administration
- Data is transmitted securely via HTTPS

## Contact

If you experience technical issues or have questions about the survey, please contact your organization's administrator or HR department.
